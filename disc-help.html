Why are some discs grayed out?

To avoid copyright issues, I can’t legally use some Minecraft discs with this extension. Mojang owns them, and they don’t make those files available online.

The good news: if you already have Minecraft installed on your computer, those exact tracks are sitting on your own computer. We just need to show Chrome where they are. Look at the instructions below to get everything working.

I get it — this extra setup step is annoying. But once you do it, you’ll have every disc working, and you’ll never have to touch it again.

If Minecraft is installed on this computer

Windows

  • Press Windows ✱ + R, type %AppData%\.minecraft, and hit Enter.
  • Select the assets folder (don’t open subfolders — just highlight the folder itself).
  • Back in the popup, click Use Local Minecraft Audio and pick that folder.
  • If you can’t see it, enable View → Hidden items in File Explorer.

macOS

  • Open Finder and press Command ⌘ + Shift + G.
  • Paste ~/Library/Application Support/minecraft and hit Enter.
  • Find the folder named assets (don’t open it).
  • Go back to the popup → click Use Local Minecraft Audio → choose that assets folder.
  • If you don’t see it, press Command ⌘ + Shift + . to show hidden folders.

Chrome might also ask for access to an objects folder and a JSON file in indexes. That’s normal — it’s just the extension reading your local music files. You’re not uploading anything.

If you don't have minecraft installed on your computer

No problem. The big Jukebox button still works — it’ll play a randomized mix of newer Minecraft tracks right away.

The extension still has a lot of the other discs available. You can listen to any of them even without Minecraft installed. The new "Jukebox" feature will let you enjoy a randomized mix of over 40 new tracks.
