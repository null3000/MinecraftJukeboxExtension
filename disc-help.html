Why are some discs grayed out?

To avoid copyright issues, I can’t legally use some Minecraft discs with this extension. Mojang owns them, and they don’t make those files available online.

The good news: if you already have Minecraft installed on your computer, those exact tracks are sitting on your own computer. We just need to show Chrome where they are. Look at the instructions below to get everything working.

I get it — this extra setup step is annoying. But once you do it, you’ll have every disc working, and you’ll never have to touch it again.

If Minecraft (Java Edition) is installed on your computer

Windows

  • Open the popup and click Use Local Minecraft Audio. A folder picker appears.
  • Inside that picker press Ctrl + L, paste %AppData%\\.minecraft, and hit Enter.
  • Highlight the assets folder (don’t drill inside) and click Select Folder.
  • If it’s missing, enable View → Hidden items in File Explorer, then try again.

macOS

  • Open the popup and click Use Local Minecraft Audio. When the picker opens, press Command ⌘ + Shift + G.
  • Paste ~/Library/Application Support/minecraft and press Enter.
  • Select the assets folder (keep it closed) and click Open.
  • If you can’t see it, press Command ⌘ + Shift + . once to reveal hidden folders.

Chrome might also ask for access to an objects folder and a JSON file in indexes. That’s normal — it’s just the extension reading your local music files.

If you don't have Minecraft installed on your computer

No problem. The big Jukebox button still works — it’ll play a randomized mix of newer Minecraft tracks right away.

The extension still has a lot of the other discs available. You can listen to many of them even without Minecraft installed. The new "Jukebox" feature will let you enjoy a newly added randomized mix of over 40 tracks from the game.
